MATH

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantity, and space, a science of structure, order, and relation that has evolved from elementary practices like counting and measuring. Its development involves logical reasoning and the increasing abstraction of its subject matter, with modern math exploring abstract entities defined by axioms. The field is broadly divided into pure mathematics, which explores abstract concepts for their own sake, and applied mathematics, which uses mathematical principles to solve real-world problems.


SCIENCE

Science is the process of systematically using observation and experimentation to gain knowledge about the natural and social world. It is both a body of knowledge and a dynamic process of discovery, which organizes this knowledge through testable hypotheses and predictions. Science is broadly divided into natural sciences (like physics and biology), social sciences (like psychology), and formal sciences (like mathematics).

GEOMETRY

Geometry is the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties of space, including shape, size, and the relative position of figures. It is one of the oldest mathematical sciences, originally developed from practical problems like surveying, and is used today in fields ranging from physics to computer science. Key concepts include points, lines, angles, surfaces, and solids.
